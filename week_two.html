Sprint Retrospective

1. What went well

What went well in this week’s sprint was that the interviews went smoothly. I was able to complete most of them on Monday and early Tuesday, with four participants in total. From my experience with interviews last term, I felt more confident in the process and knew what I was looking for. The questions I asked led to really useful insights and helped shape the direction of my project. I was able to identify patterns, frustrations, and possible areas for solutions based on what participants shared.

2. What didn't go well

What didn’t go well was the ideation stage. During my desk research, I struggled to find existing competitors that directly address the problem I’m focusing on. Most of what I found was loosely related, but nothing specific. This made it harder to come up with solution ideas because I had less to reference or compare with.

3. What can be improved

Next time, I want to move more quickly from insights to sketching possible ideas, even if they’re rough. I spent too long trying to find a “perfect” reference or example, when I could have started visualising my own concepts earlier. For the upcoming low-fidelity prototyping sprint, I’ll focus on turning key insights into quick, testable ideas instead of overthinking them.

4. What actions will I take for the next sprint

My focus going into the next sprint will be turning my insights into clear design ideas. I’ll start sketching and building low-fidelity prototypes, and based on what I’ve learned so far, I’ll try not to overthink the first concepts. Now that my ideas and solutions are more refined, I’ll use them to guide my wireframes and keep things simple and focused. I’ll also prioritise early testing — both with users and during mentoring — so I can get useful feedback before I go too far in one direction. On top of that, I’ll aim to iterate quickly and test again through the week, so I’m better prepared for the next phase.
