Sprint Retrospective

1. What went well

What went well this week was that I was able to take a step back and look at my research from a wider perspective. Once I did that, I started to see better connections between different ideas and could use what I had already found in more meaningful ways. After doing brainstorms and the research, I felt more confident towards the end of the week with the direction (problem statement) that I was going to go with.

2. What didn't go well

I spent too much time looking into just one area. I was really focused on it, but then after seeing what other students were exploring, I realised I could try something more personal that I actually relate to. That made me step back and look at things more broadly again. I also started looking for connections between the new ideas and the research I had already done, which helped make things feel more relevant. Though, I think eventually all the research I did in the one area (environment) was really useful when finding the problem statement.

3. What can be improved

What I can improve is the way I structure my research and planning phases. I need to allow more time for open exploration before narrowing down, rather than trying to define the direction too quickly. I also need a better system for keeping track of evolving ideas. Right now, things get lost or feel disconnected. Building in short, regular check-ins with myself or through mentoring could help me stay focused while still staying open to shifts in direction.

4. What actions will I take for the next sprint

Next sprint, I will refer to my term schedule that I created and follow it accordingly. I will try to move quicker and move on with my tasks to make sure I am not falling behind with the schedule. I will also try to do mentoring earlier in the week so I get more work done and stay on track.
